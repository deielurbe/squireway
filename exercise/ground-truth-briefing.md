# Project Phoenix — What You Know

You joined Meridian two months ago. You've been in a few meetings about Project Phoenix — the company's first client conference. Here's what you know from being in the room.

**Robin**, who was coordinating the conference, left last week. The CEO just forwarded you a pile of emails and documents and asked you to figure out where things stand. The conference is about four weeks away.

Here's what you're confident about from your time on the project:

1. **Budget is €22K** — it was higher before but got revised down after the main sponsor pulled out. You've heard people mention different numbers in conversation but you know the approved budget is €22K. 4. **It's a panel discussion, not a keynote.** Three Meridian partners and one external guest. The keynote idea was dropped before you started.

5. **Target is 60 attendees** — clients only. You know it was originally planned bigger but was scaled down.

6. **The printed programs question is still open.** Someone brought it up in a meeting and nobody had an answer. As far as you know, no decision was ever made.

7. **The CEO has said, more than once, that the main goal is strengthening client relationships.** Not thought leadership, not marketing — relationships. You've heard this directly.
