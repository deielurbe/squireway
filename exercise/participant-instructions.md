# Project Phoenix — Your Task

Thomas needs to know where things stand. Build him a short slide deck — something he can flick through in 5 minutes and know exactly what's going on.

Use the Meridian slide template (`meridian-slide-template.html`) and Cowork to produce it.

## Your slides should cover:

1. **Budget** — What's the current budget? Are we on track? If not, what do you recommend?

2. **Venue & Date** — Where and when is this happening? Is it confirmed?

3. **Format & Speakers** — What's the format? Who's speaking? Is everything locked in?

4. **Deliverables Status** — Catering, invitations, programs, marketing materials — what's confirmed, what's open, what's missing?

5. **Risks & Decisions Needed** — What should Thomas be worried about? What needs his decision before the conference happens?

6. **Budget Recommendation** — We're over budget. What do you recommend cutting and why?

One slide per question. Title slide at the front. That's 7 slides total.

## How to work

- Use the template file as your starting point — tell Cowork to use it
- You decide what goes on each slide based on what you've found
- Use the highlight boxes for key information and the warning boxes for risks
- The status badges (confirmed, at risk, unknown) are there for a reason — use them honestly
- If you don't know something, say so. "Unknown" is a valid answer. Making something up is not.
